STATEMENT (Question and Answer Format)
----------------------------------------------------

Q: Please state your name, address, and how long you have lived in Springfield.
A: My name is David Okafor. I live at 708 Maple Street in Springfield. I've lived
   here about six years now.

Q: Were you present at the intersection of Oak Ridge Boulevard and 5th Avenue on
   March 14, 2024?
A: Yes. I was waiting to cross the street at the crosswalk on the corner of Oak Ridge
   and 5th, on the east side of the intersection. I was on my lunch break — I work
   about two blocks from there.

Q: Can you describe the conditions at the time of the accident?
A: The weather was clear. It was a bright afternoon, probably around 2:45. Roads were
   completely dry. Traffic was moderate — a few cars at the light.

Q: Please describe in your own words what you observed.
A: I was standing at the crosswalk, waiting for the walk signal. I noticed a white
   Honda Civic stopped at the light — it was the first car at the red light in the
   lane heading east on Oak Ridge. The light was red. It had been red for a few
   seconds already.

   Then I saw a silver sedan coming from behind — moving pretty fast. I thought it
   was going to slow down but it never did. It did not brake at all before it hit the
   Civic. The impact was loud. The Civic got pushed forward, I'd say about fifteen
   feet, maybe a bit more. The silver sedan came to a stop in the intersection.

Q: How fast do you estimate the silver sedan was traveling before impact?
A: I'm not great at judging speed, but it felt fast for a surface street. Maybe 35 to
   40 miles per hour. It definitely was not slowing down at all.

Q: What happened immediately after the collision?
A: The driver of the white Civic — a man — got out of his car. He was moving slowly,
   kind of stumbling. He was holding the back of his neck with both hands. He leaned
   against the car for a moment. He didn't look right. I called 911 right away.

Q: What did the driver of the silver sedan do?
A: The woman in the silver sedan got out too. She appeared uninjured. She walked toward
   the Civic driver. Some other people who had been in their cars also came over.

Q: What was the state of the traffic signal at the time of impact?
A: The light was red for the direction the silver sedan was traveling. I'm certain of
   that. It had been red for several seconds before the crash happened. The Civic was
   already stopped at the light.

Q: Did you see any brake lights on the silver sedan before impact?
A: No. None at all. That's what caught my attention — the car wasn't slowing down.

Q: Did you speak with either driver at the scene?
A: I briefly spoke to the Civic driver after police arrived. He said his neck and back
   hurt. I told him I had called 911 and that I had seen what happened. I gave my
   contact information to one of the officers.

Q: Is there anything else you would like to add?
A: I just want to say that the light was clearly red and the Civic was completely
   stopped. The silver sedan did not attempt to stop. I saw it all clearly from
   the crosswalk — I had a direct line of sight.
